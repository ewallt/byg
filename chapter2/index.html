📖 Behold Your God

Chapter 2: This is Eternal Life
Pages 7-15

🎯 The Point of the Chapter

Central Thesis: The author's central argument is that a true, experiential knowledge of God and of Jesus Christ is eternal life itself; this knowledge alone transforms character, imparts divine power and peace, and secures salvation, while ignorance or distortion of God's character leads inevitably to spiritual ruin and eternal death.
Life Eternal = Knowledge of God and Christ
∴ Absence of Knowledge = Death Eternal

The Life-Death Contrast

🌟 Knowledge of God Produces:

  • Character transformation
  • Divine power and peace
  • Spiritual recreation in God's image
  • Salvation and eternal life

💀 Ignorance/Distortion Results In:

  • Spiritual ruin
  • Moral degeneracy
  • Stagnant Christian experience
  • Eternal death
📜 Foundation Scripture - John 17:3 (Amplified): "And this is eternal life: [it means] to know (to perceive, recognize, become acquainted with and understand) You, the only true and real God, and [likewise] to know Him, Jesus [as the] Christ, the Anointed One, the Messiah, Whom You have sent."

📚 How the Author Makes His Case

Argument 1: Knowledge Transforms Character and Is Indispensable to Salvation

Salvation demands restoration of God's image, accomplished only through knowing Him. This knowledge is not merely intellectual but transformative and recreative.

Key Insight: "The knowledge of God is life eternal, and life eternal is the knowledge of God."
Testimonies for the Church, vol. 8, p. 289
"The knowledge of God as revealed in Christ is the knowledge that all who are saved must have. It is the knowledge that works transformation of character. This knowledge, received, will recreate the soul in the image of God. It will impart to the whole being a spiritual power that is divine."
Argument 2: Grace and Peace Are Channeled Through Knowledge of God

Divine power and blessings flow specifically through the knowledge of God. Grace and peace don't just happen—they have a definite channel.

Key Insight: "Grace and peace do not just happen; they flow through the knowledge of God."
📜 2 Peter 1:2-4: Shows grace, peace, divine power, and "all things that pertain to life and godliness" come "through the knowledge of Him."
The Great Controversy, p. 394
Grace defined as "…the regenerating, enlightening power of the Holy Spirit" - active, transforming power rather than mere attitude.
Argument 3: Rejecting Knowledge of God Produces Moral Degeneracy

Romans 1 provides a detailed case study showing humanity's moral descent when knowledge of God is suppressed or rejected.

Key Insight: "The level of morality is directly determined by the knowledge of God's character."
📜 Romans 1:18-32: Documents the progression from rejecting God's revelation to complete moral breakdown: "And even as they did not like to retain God in their knowledge, God gave them over to a debased mind..."
Argument 4: Meager or False Concepts Cripple Spiritual Experience

Even believers stagnate when their concept of God is dim or distorted. Christ's ministry—and the end-time message—corrects Satan's misrepresentations.

Transformation Challenge: "Old concepts will be challenged; many things about God will be exactly opposite from what has been believed."
Testimonies for the Church, vol. 5, p. 744
"Brethren and sisters, it is by beholding that we become changed. By dwelling upon the love of God and our Saviour, by contemplating the perfection of the divine character and claiming the righteousness of Christ as ours by faith, we are to be transformed into the same image."

💡 Key Terminology & Concepts

Knowledge of God
Accurate, relational comprehension of God's character as revealed in Christ; identical with eternal life. From the Amplified Bible: "to perceive, recognize, become acquainted with and understand." This is not mere intellectual knowledge but experiential, transformative understanding.
Grace of God
"The regenerating, enlightening power of the Holy Spirit" (GC 394). Not merely an attitude of favor, but active, transforming divine power that recreates the soul in God's image.
Peace with God
The indwelling presence of God that replaces the enmity of the carnal mind (Rom 8:7 → 5:1). True peace comes through justification by faith and knowing God's true character.
Meager / False Views of God
Distorted concepts that stifle spiritual growth and foster sin. These dim or incorrect understandings prevent believers from experiencing the fullness of God's power and peace, keeping them in spiritual infancy.
Character Transformation
The process by which knowledge of God recreates the soul in His image. This happens through beholding and contemplating God's perfect character as revealed in Christ. "It is by beholding that we become changed."

✨ Chapter Conclusion & Synthesis

🔑 The Supreme Pursuit

The chapter concludes that every dimension of Christian life—power, peace, character, destiny—rises or falls with one's grasp of God's true character. Hence the supreme pursuit of believers must be the diligent, prayerful contemplation of God as revealed in Jesus.

"To know God is life eternal, and life eternal is to know God."
The All-Encompassing Truth: This knowledge is not optional or supplementary to Christian experience—it IS Christian experience. It is not a means to eternal life—it IS eternal life. Every spiritual blessing, every divine power, every character transformation flows through this single channel: knowing God as He truly is, as revealed in Jesus Christ.
The Correction Coming: Satan has systematically misrepresented God's character throughout history. Christ came to correct these false representations, and the end-time message will complete this work. Believers must be prepared to have their concepts challenged and corrected.
